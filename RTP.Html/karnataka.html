Kambala in Karnataka

The Thrilling Buffalo Race of Karnataka

About Kambala

Kambala is a traditional buffalo race held in the coastal districts of Karnataka, especially in Mangalore and Udupi. It's conducted in slushy paddy fields where pairs of buffaloes race along a muddy track, guided by farmers.

Fast. Wild. Fierce.

A celebration of tradition and rural pride.

It's not just a sport—Kambala reflects the agrarian spirit and festive fervor of coastal Karnataka.

Why It’s Famous

Besides its unique format, Kambala showcases man-animal synergy, strength, speed, and tradition. It draws massive crowds and is a spectacle of rural celebration and community bonding.

Basic Rules

  • Pairs of buffaloes race through slushy tracks.
  • Each team has a jockey who stands on a wooden plank behind the buffaloes.
  • Races are timed; some events include water height splashes as criteria.
  • No mechanical aids—only traditional techniques and skill.

Benefits of Playing

Boosts Animal Care

Promotes better upkeep and bond with buffaloes.

Encourages Fitness

Demands agility, balance, and core strength for racers.

Preserves Heritage

Kambala is an iconic reflection of coastal Karnataka culture.
