Mallakhamb in MadyaPradesh

The Traditional Gymnastic Sport of Madhya Pradesh

About Mallakhamb

Mallakhamb is a traditional Indian sport that combines gymnastics and wrestling grips, practiced on a wooden pole or rope. Madhya Pradesh has been a major center for its promotion and practice.

Strength. Balance. Grace.

A timeless art of control and tradition.

Rooted in ancient practices, Mallakhamb exemplifies discipline, agility, and the cultural heritage of Madhya Pradesh.

Why It’s Famous

Mallakhamb is not just a sport but an extraordinary blend of strength, flexibility, and endurance. Its uniqueness lies in the fusion of athleticism and traditional training methods.

Basic Rules

  • Performed on a vertical wooden pole or hanging rope.
  • Athletes execute poses and transitions with balance and control.
  • Scored on strength, flexibility, technique, and presentation.
  • No external support—entirely based on physical mastery.

Benefits of Playing

Enhances Flexibility

Improves body coordination, joint mobility, and range of motion.

Builds Core Strength

Demands immense control, strength, and muscular endurance.

Cultural Identity

Mallakhamb is a cultural pride of Madhya Pradesh and India.

Mallakhamb stands tall as a pillar of strength, tradition, and timeless Indian heritage.
