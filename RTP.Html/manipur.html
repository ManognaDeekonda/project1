About Polo

Polo is the official state sport of Manipur, and it holds a special place in the culture and heritage of the region. Known as "Sagol Kangjei" in Manipuri, it has been played for centuries, dating back to the 15th century.

Manipur is considered the birthplace of modern polo, with the sport evolving from a traditional game played by the Manipuri kings to a globally recognized sport. It involves skillful horsemanship and strategy, making it an exciting and prestigious game.

History of Polo

The history of polo in Manipur dates back over 500 years. Originally, it was played as a royal sport, enjoyed by the kings and the elite class. Over time, the sport spread to other regions and was modernized, but its roots remain deeply embedded in Manipur.

It was in the early 19th century that polo became an organized sport in Manipur. The Manipuri kings used it as a means of physical training for their soldiers, while also emphasizing the importance of teamwork and strategy.

Today, Manipur is globally recognized for its contribution to the sport, with its polo clubs and teams regularly competing in international tournaments.

Popularity

Polo is a highly popular sport in Manipur, with a deep connection to the local culture and traditions. It is a prominent feature in many cultural and religious festivals, with grand polo tournaments held annually.

The state's enthusiasm for polo is reflected in the numerous polo grounds, training centers, and clubs across Manipur. The sport is especially popular among the youth, who are eager to participate in the rich legacy of polo.

Government support, along with a growing interest in the international polo scene, has ensured that polo remains a key part of Manipur's cultural and sporting identity.

Rules and Regulations

Polo is a team sport played between two teams of four players each, who ride on horseback. The objective is to score goals by hitting a small ball into the opposing team's goal using a mallet.

  • Team Structure: Each team consists of four players: a back, a mid-player, and two forwards. The players use mallets to hit the ball, aiming to score goals.
  • Duration: Polo matches are played in periods called chukkers. Typically, a polo match lasts for 4-6 chukkers, each lasting 7 minutes.

Benefits

Participating in polo offers numerous advantages:

Physical Fitness

Polo enhances cardiovascular health, coordination, and muscle strength. It requires skillful horsemanship, making it a physically demanding sport.

Mental Strategy

The game requires strategic thinking, teamwork, and quick decision-making, sharpening mental acuity and focus.

Cultural Legacy

Preserving and promoting polo connects the youth of Manipur with their rich cultural heritage, fostering pride and identity.

These benefits have helped make polo not only a source of physical fitness but also a cultural symbol for the people of Manipur.

Promoting polo in Manipur helps preserve its cultural roots and fosters a sense of community, while also offering physical and mental fitness opportunities.
