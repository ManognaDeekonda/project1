The World of Kabaddi

An ancient Indian sport that combines strength, strategy, and agility

History of Kabaddi

Kabaddi is an ancient sport that originated in India over 4,000 years ago. The game was mentioned in the Mahabharata, one of the most revered Hindu epics. Its rich history makes it one of the oldest sports still played in its original form today.

Rules and Regulations

  • Objective: The objective of Kabaddi is for a player to enter the opponent's half of the field and tag as many opponents as possible without being tackled.
  • Gameplay: The game is played with two teams of seven players each. Players take turns sending a "raider" to the other team's side to tag opponents and return to their own side.
  • Scoring: Points are scored when a raider tags one or more opponents and returns to their own side without being tackled.

Benefits

  • Physical Fitness: Kabaddi improves physical fitness, agility, and endurance through its dynamic movements and continuous action.
  • Teamwork: The game develops teamwork and strategy among players as both offense and defense require coordination.
  • Mental Toughness: Kabaddi enhances mental toughness and focus as players must make split-second decisions under pressure.

Popularity

Kabaddi has gained immense popularity in recent years, with professional leagues like the Pro Kabaddi League (PKL) in India revolutionizing the sport's visibility. The game has also been included in international multi-sport events like the Asian Games, bringing it to a global audience.
