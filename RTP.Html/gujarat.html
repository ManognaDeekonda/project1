History of Kho Kho in Gujarat

Kho Kho, with its origins rooted in ancient India, is passionately played in Gujarat, symbolizing speed, strategy, and teamwork. It was popularized in schools and rural communities as an exciting team game requiring quick reflexes and smart dodging.

Popularity

Kho Kho is widely played in both rural and urban Gujarat. It's a key part of school curriculums and district-level events, especially in tribal and semi-urban areas where local tournaments draw large crowds.

The game encourages children to participate due to its minimal equipment needs and team-based excitement.

Rules and Regulations

Kho Kho follows rules governed by the Kho Kho Federation of India. The standard format includes:

  • Team Formation: Played between two teams of 12 players (9 active, 3 reserves).
  • Chasing and Dodging: One team chases while the other defends by running and dodging around a narrow rectangular field.

Chasers pass the pursuit by touching teammates who sit in a line alternately facing opposite directions. Strategic direction changes and timing are vital.
